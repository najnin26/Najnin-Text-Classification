## Abstract
The natural language processing field requires sentiment analysis and text classification as essential functions, yet deep learning models from the past struggle to handle complex feature interactions with their existing modeling methods. To address this limitation, this paper proposes <strong>QDeBERTa-BiLSTM</strong>, a solution that employs a hybrid quantum-classical framework that integrates DeBERTa-based contextual embeddings, BiLSTM sequence modeling, and variational quantum circuits to enhance feature representation. The suggested model employs quantum circuits to represent nonlinear interactions while maintaining high contextual knowledge via transformer architectures. Multiple experiments are executed on benchmark datasets such as Spam, Movie Reviews (MR), and MPQA. The model outperforms several previous hybrid and classical baselines, achieving <strong>97.13\%</strong> accuracy on Spam and around <strong>86\%</strong> accuracy on both MR and MPQA. Furthermore, various quantum circuit designs are tested, revealing that circuit design has a major impact on performance. The results highlight the effectiveness of combining classical and quantum techniques for improved text classification.
</HighlightedSection>

## Methodology
<section class="sec" id="methodology">
  <div class="col">
    <h4>Embedding Representation</h4>
    <p>Each token embedding is formed by summing three components — subword, absolute positional, and relative positional:</p>
    <div class="eq">$$E = E_{token} + E_{abs} + E_{rel}$$</div>

    <h4>DeBERTa — Disentangled Attention</h4>
    <p>DeBERTa separates content and position information. The attention score between tokens $W_i$ and $W_j$ is computed as:</p>
    <div class="eq">$$\text{Attention}(W_i, W_j) = \text{Softmax}\!\left(\frac{V_{W_i}^Q\!\left(V_{W_j}^K\right)^{\!T}}{\sqrt{d_k}}\right)V_{W_j}^V$$</div>

    <h4>BiLSTM — Bidirectional Sequence Modeling</h4>
    <p>The BiLSTM processes sequences in both forward and backward directions, concatenating the hidden states:</p>
    <div class="eq">$$\overrightarrow{h_t}=\text{LSTM}_f(H_t) \qquad \overleftarrow{h_t}=\text{LSTM}_b(H_t) \qquad h_t=\bigl[\overrightarrow{h_t};\,\overleftarrow{h_t}\bigr]$$</div>

    <h4>Quantum State Initialization</h4>
    <p>Qubits start in the ground state. Hadamard gates create superposition:</p>
    <div class="eq">$$|\psi_0\rangle=|0\rangle^{\otimes q} \qquad |\psi_1\rangle=H^{\otimes q}|\psi_0\rangle$$</div>

    <h4>Quantum Transformation &amp; Measurement</h4>
    <p>Classical features are angle-encoded via parameterized rotations. The variational unitary $U(\theta)$ is optimized jointly with the classical network. Pauli-$Z$ expectation values form the quantum feature vector:</p>
    <div class="eq">$$|\psi(\theta)\rangle=U(\theta)|\psi_1\rangle \qquad z_i=\langle\psi(\theta)|Z_i|\psi(\theta)\rangle \qquad \mathbf{z}=[z_1,\ldots,z_q]$$</div>

    <h4>Computational Complexity</h4>
    <div class="tw">
      <table>
        <thead>
          <tr><th>Component</th><th>Complexity</th><th>Notes</th></tr>
        </thead>
        <tbody>
          <tr>
            <td>DeBERTa multi-head attention</td>
            <td>$O(n^2 \cdot d)$</td>
            <td>Dominant cost; $n$ = sequence length, $d$ = hidden dimension</td>
          </tr>
          <tr>
            <td>BiLSTM (forward + backward)</td>
            <td>$O(n \cdot h^2)$</td>
            <td>Linear in sequence length</td>
          </tr>
          <tr>
            <td>Variational quantum circuit</td>
            <td>$O(q \cdot L)$</td>
            <td>Minimal at $q{=}2, L{=}2$</td>
          </tr>
        </tbody>
      </table>
    </div>

<section class="sec" id="circuits">
  <div class="col">
    <h2>Quantum Circuit Designs</h2>
    <p>
      Four variational quantum circuit architectures are explored, each exposing different combinations of rotation depth,
      entanglement strategy, and controlled operations — allowing a systematic study of how circuit design impacts downstream classification.
    </p>
    <div class="grid grid-cols-2 gap-6 mt-6">
      <div class="qc bg-gray-800 text-gray-100 p-4 rounded-lg shadow-md">
        <span class="qb qb1">QC1</span>
        <div class="qt font-semibold text-blue-400">Entangling Circuit</div>
        <p class="qd text-sm text-gray-300 mt-2">
          Hadamard initialization → RX/RY rotations → CNOT entanglement between adjacent qubits, enabling inter-qubit correlation discovery.
        </p>
        <div class="qstar mt-2 text-yellow-400 text-xs">⭐ Best overall — most stable across all datasets</div>
        <div class="eq bg-gray-900 text-white p-3 rounded-lg text-center mt-3 text-xs">
          $$U_{C1}(\theta)=\prod_{i=1}^{q-1}\!\text{CNOT}_{i,i+1}\cdot\prod_{i=1}^{q}R_Y(\theta_i^y)R_X(\theta_i^x)$$
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="qc bg-gray-800 text-gray-100 p-4 rounded-lg shadow-md">
        <span class="qb qb2">QC2</span>
        <div class="qt font-semibold text-blue-400">Rotation-Based Circuit</div>
        <p class="qd text-sm text-gray-300 mt-2">
          Single-qubit RX and RY rotations only — no entangling gates. Each qubit evolves independently, encoding classical features into quantum amplitudes.
        </p>
        <div class="eq bg-gray-900 text-white p-3 rounded-lg text-center mt-3 text-xs">
          $$U_{C2}(\theta)=\prod_{i=1}^{q}R_Y(\theta_i^y)R_X(\theta_i^x)$$
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="qc bg-gray-800 text-gray-100 p-4 rounded-lg shadow-md">
        <span class="qb qb3">QC3</span>
        <div class="qt font-semibold text-blue-400">Layered Rotation Circuit</div>
        <p class="qd text-sm text-gray-300 mt-2">
          Multiple stacked layers of RX → RY → RZ on every qubit. Greater circuit depth increases expressibility for complex feature transformations.
        </p>
        <div class="eq bg-gray-900 text-white p-3 rounded-lg text-center mt-3 text-xs">
          $$U_{C3}(\theta)=\prod_{l=1}^{L}\prod_{i=1}^{q}R_Z(\theta_{i,l}^z)R_Y(\theta_{i,l}^y)R_X(\theta_{i,l}^x)$$
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="qc bg-gray-800 text-gray-100 p-4 rounded-lg shadow-md">
        <span class="qb qb4">QC4</span>
        <div class="qt font-semibold text-blue-400">Controlled Operation Circuit</div>
        <p class="qd text-sm text-gray-300 mt-2">
          RX/RY/RZ rotations combined with CNOT-based controlled operations, enhancing inter-qubit information transfer for complex feature modeling.
        </p>
        <div class="qstar mt-2 text-yellow-400 text-xs">⭐ Best on MPQA — superior for sentiment-intensive tasks</div>
        <div class="eq bg-gray-900 text-white p-3 rounded-lg text-center mt-3 text-xs">
          $$U_{C4}(\theta)=\prod_{i=1}^{q-1}\!\text{CNOT}_{i,i+1}\cdot\prod_{i=1}^{q}R_Z(\theta_i^z)R_Y(\theta_i^y)R_X(\theta_i^x)$$
        </div>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div class="col">
    <h2>Conclusion</h2>
    <p>QDeBERTa-BiLSTM demonstrates that integrating variational quantum circuits into a classical transformer-LSTM pipeline yields meaningful gains for text classification. The model achieves <strong>97%</strong> on Spam and <strong>86%</strong> on both MR and MPQA — surpassing all prior hybrid quantum-classical baselines.</p>
    <p>The circuit design study reveals a clear tradeoff: entangling circuits (QC1) offer stable, consistent performance across all tasks, while controlled-operation circuits (QC4) excel on nuanced sentiment classification (MPQA). Current limitations include reliance on PennyLane simulators, a small qubit count ($q = 2$), and NISQ-era hardware constraints. Future work will target real quantum hardware, more expressive circuit designs, and extended multilingual and domain-specific benchmarks.</p>

</section>
